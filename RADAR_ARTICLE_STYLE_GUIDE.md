## RADAR Article Style Guide

### Purpose
Keep Know How and practical articles readable, scannable, and useful without turning them into noisy content blocks.

### Core Principle
RADAR articles should not read like soft essays.
They should feel like clear editorial explainers with visible signal:
- strong hook
- obvious structure
- practical usefulness
- memorable phrasing
- calm emphasis

### Default Pattern For Future Practical Articles
Use this as the usual baseline, not as a rigid checklist:
- 1 strong opening hook
- 1 `Nejdůležitější:` moment
- 1 practical `:::warning`
- 1 real-life `:::example`
- 1 `Co si odnést:` summary
- 1 final `:::takeaway`

### Emphasis Rules
- Use `**bold**` for the most important phrase in a paragraph, not the whole paragraph.
- Use `==inline highlight==` only for the phrase the eye should catch on scroll.
- Use `Nejdůležitější:` when one sentence frames the whole section.
- Use `V jedné větě:` when a concept benefits from a fast compression.
- Use `Co si odnést:` when the reader should be able to stop and keep the point.

### Supported Content Blocks
- `:::term` for simple definitions or mini explainers
- `:::warning` for common mistakes or expensive illusions
- `:::tip` for better framing or next-step thinking
- `:::example` for real-life scenarios
- `:::takeaway` for the closing signal
- short tables when comparison genuinely helps

### Tone Rules
- smart
- practical
- slightly ironic where useful
- respectful
- no cringe
- no school vibe
- no finance-guru tone

### Anti-Clutter Rules
- not every section needs a box
- not every paragraph needs bold
- do not stack callouts back to back unless the article really needs it
- use tables only when comparison is faster than prose
- if a block does not increase scan value, cut it

### Good Outcome
When someone scrolls fast, they should immediately catch:
- the main idea
- the main warning
- the practical example
- the one thing to remember
